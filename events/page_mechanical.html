Knock On(Chain Reaction)

Get the best chain of creativity and ideas.

Description

The “Knock On” is a contraption event, which involves different 'ENERGY CONVERSION' steps. The term is best known as ‘Rube Goldberg Mechanism’. The Rube Goldberg Mechanism is a chain of response that occurs when a small change causes a similar change nearby, which then causes another similar change, and so on in sequence, leading to completion of final task.

This event requires simple class room ideas along with innovation that can make the solution as complicated and fascinating as possible. Throw in all the energy conversions you are aware of, as one continuous routine without physically engaging yourself at any stage. All you need is planning, innovation, efficiency in steps and best use of material to stay in the competition.

Rules :

  • A team may consist of maximum 4 members. Students from different institutes can form a team.
  • Every participant must get himself/herself registered before the event begins.
  • No damage should be made to the materials that are provided to the participants.
  • Participants are not allowed to use any external material other than provided.
  • Each team must formulate their own ideas and designs. Any form of plagiarism will lead to disqualification.
  • Judge's decision will be final and binding on all.
  • Any fraud on the part of the participating team, once detected, will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • The number of rounds and their contents are liable to change before the event and the change will be informed to all who registered.
  • Event organizers has all rights to change or add new rules.
