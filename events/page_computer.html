Relay Coding

Get your "Codemate"

Description

Relay Coding is the programming event which mainly focuses on the programming and ability to understand the Coding between 2 participants.

Environemeny

  • Platform: Ubuntu.
  • Tools: Code blocks, Gedit
  • Compiler: gcc
  • Language: C/C++

Round 1 :

  1. Statement will be given at the time of event.
  2. Time Limit: 30 minutes.
  3. In 30 minutes,
    1. First 10 minutes will be given to First participant
    2. Next 10 minutes will be given to second participant to complete the remaining code
    3. Remaining 10 minutes, both can code together.

Round 2 :

  1. Time Limit: 60 minutes.
  2. In 60 minutes,
    1. First 30 minutes will be given to First participant
    2. Next 30 minutes will be given to second participant to complete the remaining code

Evaluation Criteria

  1. Coordination
  2. Problem Completion Level
  3. Logic

Rules and Regulations :

  1. Team Size: Exact 2 participants
  2. Event Time Duration
    1. 30 minutes (Preliminary Round)
    2. 60 minutes (Final Round)
  3. The pre-requisite for participation in this event is sound knowledge of the C/C++ Programming.
  4. The participants should report at the venue 15 minutes before the scheduled time of the event.
  5. Internet usage will not be allowed to the participants.
  6. If lesser number of teams register for this event, there will be only final round.
  7. If any rules are found to be broken by any of the team member, it will lead to their disqualification.
  8. The decision of the event organizers would be final and shall not to be subject to any change.

C-Quiz and Debugging

C-atechism Amend.

Description

  1. Participation: Individual
  2. About Event: Competition to be held in two rounds.
  3. Preliminary: 30 minutes for 30 MCQ’s.
  4. Finals: (45 minutes for 3 Questions)
  5. Platform : Ubuntu
  6. Tools provided: cc, gcc, Code blocks.

Round 1 :

  1. Round 1 has 30 multiple choice questions (MCQs).
  2. Top 25 participants go further for second (final) round.
  3. Marks and Time will be considered into shortlisting for final round.

Round 2 :

  1. Final round consists of code debugging performed on computer only.
  2. In this round, program will be given with some errors in it which need to be resolved in given duration.
  3. Suitable editors will be provided.
  4. Evaluation will be based on minimum time and no of errors removed at the end.

Rules & Regulations :

  1. Organizer has the rights to change any rules or evaluation criteria, if needed.
  2. Each participant must have the identity card of his/her respected institute.
  3. Final decision is under the authority. In any circumstances, it cannot be uttered.
  4. Late reporting time will be disqualified.
  5. Participant must reach to the specified place before 15 minutes.
  6. No smart devices or Internet are allowed.
  7. If any misbehaviour or indiscipline will be noted from the participants then they will be disqualified from the event immediately.

E-Treasure Hunt(Zodiac)

Put your Googling skills to the test!

Description

Connect dozens of images, decipher impossible riddles and find patterns where none seem to exist- Zodiac is a contest where players compete to find the answer to the given puzzle, using anything and everything they can find on the Internet. Got what it takes? It is a mind stretching event.

Round 1 :

  • Solving Riddles and Logical Reasoning (Pen & Paper Round).

Round 2 :

  • Solving System Flaws (Pen & Paper Round).

Round 3 :

  • Visualization Puzzle (Goggling).

Elimination based on

  • Fastest Fingers First
  • The team that completes the entire levels will be declared the winner.

Rules & Regulations :

  • Time limit will be strictly monitored according to event start time. Any violation of the same will lead to immediate disqualification without notice.
  • The answer can range from a word to a phrase or may completely and utterly surprise you.
  • Kindly submit your answer before the time runs out.
  • No clarifications or doubts about the questions will be entertained.
  • In case of any dispute, the decision of the organizers will be final.
  • No discussion between the participants is allowed once the event starts; otherwise the organizers have the full authority to disqualify the participants.
